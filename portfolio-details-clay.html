Clay-Based Dental Coatings

This research explores the use of local Ghanaian clay to develop cost-effective and biocompatible coatings for dental applications. Through SEM, XRD, FTIR and TGA/DSC analysis, I compared the microstructural, chemical and thermal properties of clay samples with commercial dental coatings, conducting acute toxicity and antimicrobial resistance assessments to ensure safety.Two of the clay samples shared similar properties(elemental, functional groups, crystallographic planes and good thermal stability) with the commercial samples after Characterization analysis This project aims to reduce reliance on imported materials and improve accessibility in oral healthcare especially in Ghana.

Ongoing work includes optimizing the sintering process, refining surface functionalization for enhanced bioactivity, and evaluating mechanical performance of the clay materials.
