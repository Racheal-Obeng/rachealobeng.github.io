Symptom Analysis: Malaria vs COVID-19

As a co-author, I contributed to the investigation of malaria burden and its epidemiological overlap with COVID-19 in Southern Ghana. Utilizing a community health education and medical screening (CHEMS) approach, we analyzed clinical and demographic data from 994 participants to assess malaria diagnosis, treatment patterns, and co-infections with SARS-CoV-2. My role involved data analysis and interpretation, particularly in assessing malaria prevalence in relation to COVID-19 symptoms and vaccination status. The findings emphasized the significance of community-driven health interventions in mitigating dual disease burdens. This research provides valuable insights into disease co-management strategies, reinforcing the importance of sustained malaria surveillance despite pandemic challenges.
